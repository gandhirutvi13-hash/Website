GERIATRIC & SENIOR MOBILITY

Maintaining independence and confidence at home.

Loss of balance, frequent falls, or a gradual decline in walking capacity can significantly impact an older adult's quality of life. Home physiotherapy focuses on functional strength, balance training, and creating a safer home environment to support continued independence.

Discuss Senior Mobility

This service may be relevant if

  • ✓ There has been a recent fall or near-fall at home.
  • ✓ Getting up from a chair or bed has become increasingly difficult.
  • ✓ Walking distance or confidence has noticeably decreased.
  • ✓ You or a family member is recovering from a prolonged hospital stay.
  • ✓ You want a structured program to maintain muscle mass and bone health.

What the physiotherapist may assess

  • → Balance, gait and fall-risk factors.
  • → Lower body strength, particularly for sit-to-stand movements.
  • → Joint mobility and arthritis-related stiffness.
  • → Use of walking aids (sticks, walkers) and whether they are appropriate.
  • → Home environment safety (rugs, lighting, stair rails).
