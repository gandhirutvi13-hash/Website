FROZEN SHOULDER & STIFFNESS

When reaching overhead or behind your back is restricted.

Shoulder stiffness, particularly frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis), progresses through stages of pain and severe restriction. Physiotherapy aims to manage pain in the early phases and restore range of motion and strength as the joint thaws.

Discuss Shoulder Pain

This service may be relevant if

  • ✓ You have gradually losing the ability to lift or rotate your arm.
  • ✓ Shoulder pain frequently wakes you up at night.
  • ✓ Everyday tasks like dressing, washing hair or reaching a seatbelt are difficult.
  • ✓ You have been diagnosed with adhesive capsulitis or a rotator cuff issue.
  • ✓ You need guidance on which exercises are safe for your stage of stiffness.

What the physiotherapist may assess

  • → Active and passive range of motion of the shoulder joint.
  • → Pain triggers, resting pain, and night pain patterns.
  • → Neck and upper back mobility contributing to the issue.
  • → Rotator cuff strength and scapular (shoulder blade) control.
  • → Differentiating frozen shoulder from other impingement or tear issues.
