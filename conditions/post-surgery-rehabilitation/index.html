POST-SURGERY REHABILITATION

Rebuilding strength and movement after surgery.

Returning home after an orthopaedic procedure is only the first step. Proper rehabilitation requires progressive load, mobility work and guidance that respects your surgeon's protocol.

Discuss Rehab at Home

This service may be relevant if

  • ✓ You have recently had a knee or hip replacement.
  • ✓ You are recovering from a fracture repair or spinal surgery.
  • ✓ You have undergone ligament (e.g. ACL) or meniscus surgery.
  • ✓ You need help transitioning from walking aids to independent movement.
  • ✓ Travelling to a clinic is difficult or painful in the early stages of recovery.

What the physiotherapist may assess

  • → Your current mobility, walking pattern and use of walking aids.
  • → Range of motion, muscle strength and joint stability.
  • → Swelling, wound healing progress and pain levels.
  • → Your surgeon’s operative notes and rehabilitation guidelines.
  • → Safe movement around your home environment (stairs, getting in/out of bed).
